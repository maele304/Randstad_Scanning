Implications

Three takeaways from the data.

01

Tactical reduction has been substantial over 2024–2026.

−32% in 3P spend over two years. The largest absolute reductions sit in upper-funnel (mass media, brand) and lower-funnel (job boards, performance) categories — both already trending down vs Budget and vs 2025 Actual. The remaining levers identified in available materials are structural: supplier consolidation (Tab 04), production routing (Tab 05), and operating-model change (Tab 06).

€174M → €115M · 2024A to 2026F
02

The savings narrative and the growth narrative are quantified separately.

Future Services Catalogue identifies a €32M Gross Profit opportunity from organic search on €0.4M of current SEO spend — a ~55:1 GP-to-spend ratio. No equivalent ROI ratio is published for SEA (€6.9M) or Job Boards (€28.4M). The growth case sits in a different document from the savings cases.

€0.4M SEO spend → €32M GP opportunity (3yr, top 10 OpCos)
03

The three business cases are sequential, not competing.

The 2023 Original BuCa (Accenture-led) identified €32.4M end-state across three levers. Two levers have since been operationalized by Randstad teams: Content Studio BuCa deepens Lever 2 (€8.9M → €19.6M, adding a revenue dimension), and TOM27 Analysis is the in-progress deep-dive on Lever 3. Lever 1 (Brand Rationalization, €10.2M) has no operational deep-dive yet.

Asset volume distribution · observation

Banners, GIFs and copy items together account for 94% of total Factory output (12,650 of 13,454 assets). All three are high-volume, high-standardisation, high-rate-arbitrage categories — exactly the disposition labelled "Factory" in the heatmap below.

Videos at 768 (5.7%) sit in a different category: video adaptations are Factory-suitable, but longer-form video originals are Strategic. The breakdown between <30s adaptations and >30s originals is not recorded in the source data — a question for the next-phase diagnostic.

Reading the three numbers correctly

The €32.4M (Original BuCa), €19.6M (Content Studio) and €0–0.9M (TOM27) are not three independent savings cases. The Content Studio BuCa supersedes Lever 2 of the Original BuCa (€8.9M → €19.6M, the increase coming from a new revenue-effectiveness dimension). TOM27 is the in-progress operational instantiation of Lever 3 (€13.3M target).

The current view of total end-state value, with the deep-dives substituted in where they exist: Lever 1 €10.2M (Accenture estimate) + Lever 2 €19.6M (Content Studio, Scen 3A) + Lever 3 €13.3M (Accenture estimate; TOM27 completion pending) = ~€43M end-state. The number is illustrative, not committed — but it's a cleaner read than treating the three documents as competing.

Sizing rule for the next pass

Benchmark percentages apply to addressable spend, not gross spend. Accenture's Content Factory benchmark of 20-50% cost savings, applied to €115M total 3P, would imply €23-57M — but only the ~€9.3M PDCC line (or ~€31.4M FC01 content/agency bucket, depending on scope) is genuinely addressable by a Content Factory intervention. The same logic applies to FTE: applying a 20% labour reduction to all 466 FTEs overstates the pool; the pure-marketing baseline after Hygiene reclassification is ~327 FTEs.
